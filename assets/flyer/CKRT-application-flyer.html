Connecticut KUH Research Training Program

Mentored research training in kidney, urologic, and hematologic disease — CKRT

CKRT recruits trainees of exceptional promise — predoctoral students, postdoctoral physician-scientists, and postdoctoral PhD scientists — pursuing research-focused careers in nephrology, classical (benign) hematology, or benign urology.

Who can apply

  • Predoctoral students and postdoctoral fellows pursuing intensive research training in nephrology, classical (benign) hematology, or benign urology.
  • Mentorship: affiliation with Yale or UConn Health is not required, but every trainee must have a primary research mentor at Yale or UConn Health.
  • Career stage: applicants must hold a doctoral degree (PhD, MD, or DO) or be enrolled in a doctoral-degree–granting program.
  • Citizenship: TL1 appointment is limited to U.S. citizens and permanent residents (green-card holders), per NIH NRSA policy.
  • A commitment to a full-time, mentored research training experience.

Application materials

Part I — Applicant

  • General applicant information
  • NIH biosketch or CV (no specified format)
  • Personal statement (½–1 page)
  • Research plan (500 words, excl. references/figures)
  • Training plan (½–1 page)
  • Graduate transcripts (predoctoral applicants and PhD-track postdocs)

Part II — Mentor / mentorship team

  • NIH biosketch of the primary mentor (no specified format)
  • One letter of support from the mentor(s) / mentorship team; multiple mentors may sign (2-page limit)
  • List of current funding sources

How to submit

Email all materials as PDF attachments to apply@ckrt.org
  1. Combine your applicant materials (Part I) into a single PDF.
  2. Have your mentor send the mentorship materials (Part II) to the same address.
  3. Name files LastName_FirstName_Document — e.g., Doe_Jane_PersonalStatement.pdf.

Applications are accepted in response to our annual request for applications. Email apply@ckrt.org to be notified when the next cycle opens.
